Emergency Help

If you are in immediate danger, call the police at 10111 or the Gender-Based Violence Command Centre at 0800 428 428.

Understanding the Domestic Violence Act

The Domestic Violence Act 116 of 1998 provides protection for individuals experiencing domestic violence.

The Act recognizes various forms of abuse, including:

  • Physical abuse - assault, infliction of physical harm
  • Sexual abuse - non-consensual sexual conduct
  • Emotional and psychological abuse - verbal abuse, degradation
  • Economic abuse - financial control, withholding resources
  • Harassment and stalking - unwanted contact, following

How to Get a Protection Order

A protection order is a legal document that helps protect you from an abuser. Here's how to obtain one:

  1. Visit a Magistrate's Court in your area during office hours.
  2. Complete necessary forms with the help of the clerk of the court.
  3. Provide an affidavit detailing the abuse you've experienced.
  4. Receive an interim protection order for immediate safety.
  5. Attend a court hearing for a final decision on your protection order.

Important Considerations

Protection orders are legally enforceable throughout South Africa.

  • If violated, the offender can be arrested immediately.
  • Warrants of arrest can be issued alongside protection orders.
  • The Act ensures full enforcement by police and other legal bodies.
  • You have the right to bring someone with you for support.
  • Court proceedings can be held in private to protect your dignity.
