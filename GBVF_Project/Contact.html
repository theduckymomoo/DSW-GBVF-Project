About Ukhuseleko

Creating a South Africa free from domestic violence through education, advocacy, support services, and empowerment initiatives.

Our Purpose

Our Mission

At Ukhuseleko, we are dedicated to creating a South Africa free from domestic violence through education, advocacy, support services, and empowerment initiatives.

Core Values

  • Dignity: We treat all survivors with respect and without judgment
  • Justice: We work toward accountability and systems that protect the vulnerable
  • Inclusivity: Our services are accessible to everyone regardless of background
  • Empowerment: We focus on building resilience and breaking cycles of abuse

What We Do

  • Crisis Response: 24/7 helpline, emergency shelter connections, and immediate intervention
  • Education: Awareness programs in schools, communities, workplaces, and faith organizations
  • Advocacy: Working for policy reforms and improved legal responses
  • Multilingual Support: Services in isiZulu, isiXhosa, Afrikaans, English, and Sesotho

Our Impact

  • Supported 5,000+ survivors
  • Trained 200+ community advocates across all provinces
  • Educational workshops reaching 15,000+ people
  • Partnerships with 25 shelters nationwide

Ukhuseleko means "protection" in isiXhosa, reflecting our commitment to safeguarding all South Africans affected by domestic violence.
